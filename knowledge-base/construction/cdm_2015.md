# CDM 2015 — Construction (Design and Management) Regulations

## Overview
The CDM 2015 Regulations place legal duties on all parties involved in construction projects to manage health and safety. They apply to all construction work in Great Britain.

## Duty Holders and Responsibilities

### Client
- Most influence over project health and safety
- Must ensure suitable arrangements for managing the project
- Must appoint a Principal Designer and Principal Contractor on notifiable projects
- Must ensure a Construction Phase Plan and Health and Safety File are produced

### Principal Designer (PD)
- Appointed for projects with more than one contractor
- Plans, manages, monitors, and coordinates pre-construction phase H&S
- Responsible for the pre-construction Health and Safety File
- Ensures designers comply with duties

### Principal Contractor (PC)
- Takes over from PD at construction phase
- Plans, manages, monitors, and coordinates construction phase H&S
- Prepares and maintains the Construction Phase Plan (CPP)
- Runs the site — responsible for all worker welfare

### Designers
- Must consider H&S during design
- Eliminate, reduce, or control foreseeable risks in their designs
- Provide information about residual risks to help with Construction Phase Plan

### Contractors
- Plan, manage, and monitor their own work for H&S
- Comply with the CPP
- Ensure workers have site induction and necessary information

## Notifiable Projects
A project is notifiable to the HSE if construction phase is likely to:
- Last longer than **30 working days** AND have more than 20 workers simultaneously; OR
- Exceed **500 person-days** of construction work

Notify via HSE's F10 online notification portal.

## Construction Phase Plan (CPP)
Must cover:
1. Description of project and site
2. Management structure and responsibilities
3. Arrangements for H&S management
4. Principal Contractor site rules
5. Arrangements for managing significant risks (excavations, working at height, demolition)
6. Emergency and first aid arrangements

## Health and Safety File
- Produced by Principal Designer and handed to Client at project end
- Contains O&M information, as-built details, residual risks for future maintenance

## Working at Height — WAH Regulations 2005
- All work at height must be properly planned and supervised
- Hierarchy: Avoid → Prevent → Minimise
- Suitable equipment: scaffolding, MEWPs, harness systems
- Fragile surfaces — additional protection required

## Common H&S Issues on Construction Sites
1. Falls from height (leading cause of fatalities)
2. Struck by moving plant/vehicles (segregate pedestrians and plant)
3. Buried services (dial before you dig — always get service records)
4. Asbestos (check pre-demolition asbestos survey)
5. Collapse of excavations (properly shore all excavations >1.2m)
6. Manual handling injuries
