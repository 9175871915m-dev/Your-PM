# Variation Orders and Change Management

## What is a Variation Order?
A Variation Order (VO) — also called a Change Order or Compensation Event — is a formal instruction to modify the original scope of works. Every change to scope, specification, programme, or conditions must be captured formally.

## Why Change Control Matters
- Unmanaged changes are the leading cause of cost and programme overruns
- Retrospective claims are expensive and damage relationships
- Contract requirements for timely notification must be met to preserve entitlement

## Variation Order Process (Step by Step)

### Step 1: Identify the Change
- Client instruction, design development, unforeseen condition, or subcontractor proposal
- Document the trigger immediately in the Change Log

### Step 2: Early Warning / Notice
- **NEC:** Early Warning Notice within the timeframe specified in Contract Data
- **FIDIC:** Written Claim Notice within 28 days of event (Clause 20)
- **JCT:** Architect's Instruction required before proceeding

### Step 3: Assess Impact
- Programme impact: extension of time (EOT) required?
- Cost impact: material, labour, plant, prelims, subcontractor cost
- Quality/specification impact
- Prepare a Variation Assessment Form

### Step 4: Submit Quotation / Compensation Event Quotation
- Include breakdown of costs (labour hours × rate, material quantities × unit rates)
- Include programme impact (delay days, critical path effect)
- Attach supporting evidence (daywork sheets, material invoices, programme extracts)

### Step 5: Agreement and Instruction
- Client/Engineer reviews and agrees OR requests revision
- Formal instruction issued (VO signed by both parties)
- Budget and programme updated

### Step 6: Record and Monitor
- Update the Variation Register / Change Log
- Issue updated EAC (Estimate at Completion)
- Review impact on overall project contingency

## Common Causes of Variations
1. Client scope changes (most common)
2. Design errors or omissions
3. Unforeseen ground conditions (NEC CE: Clause 60.1(12))
4. Statutory authority requirements
5. Adverse weather beyond contract thresholds
6. Nominated subcontractor instructions
7. Acceleration instructions

## Preventing Variation Disputes
- Agree the valuation mechanism upfront (agreed rates schedule)
- Keep contemporaneous records (site diaries, photos, labour allocation sheets)
- Never proceed with significant changes without written instruction
- Review contract programme baseline monthly
