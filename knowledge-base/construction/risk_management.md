# Risk Management in Construction Projects

## What is a Risk Register?
A Risk Register is a formal document that captures all identified project risks, their likelihood, impact, owners, and mitigation actions. It is a living document updated throughout the project lifecycle.

## Risk Register Structure
| Column | Description |
|---|---|
| Risk ID | Unique reference (R-001, R-002…) |
| Risk Description | Clear statement of the risk event |
| Category | Schedule / Cost / Quality / Safety / Regulatory |
| Probability | 1–5 scale (1=Rare, 5=Almost Certain) |
| Impact | 1–5 scale (1=Negligible, 5=Catastrophic) |
| Risk Score | Probability × Impact |
| Response Strategy | Avoid / Transfer / Mitigate / Accept |
| Mitigation Action | Specific action to reduce likelihood/impact |
| Risk Owner | Named individual responsible |
| Status | Open / In Progress / Closed |

## Probability–Impact Matrix
A 5×5 matrix categorises risks:
- **Red (Score 15–25):** Critical — immediate action required
- **Amber (Score 8–14):** High — mitigation plan required
- **Yellow (Score 4–7):** Medium — monitor closely
- **Green (Score 1–3):** Low — accept and monitor

## Common Construction Risks
1. **Ground Conditions** — unexpected soil contamination, rock, old foundations
2. **Design Changes** — late design changes lead to variation orders and programme delays
3. **Subcontractor Default** — key subcontractor insolvency mid-project
4. **Supply Chain Disruption** — material lead times (steel, electrical gear, plant)
5. **Adverse Weather** — especially for civils and external works
6. **CDM 2015 Non-Compliance** — H&S violations and enforcement action
7. **Planning Conditions** — discharge of planning conditions delaying enabling works
8. **Utility Diversions** — unexpected utility conflicts during excavation

## Risk Response Strategies
- **Avoid:** Eliminate the risk by changing the plan (e.g., redesign to avoid contaminated ground)
- **Transfer:** Pass risk to contractor via contract clauses or insurance
- **Mitigate:** Reduce probability or impact (e.g., site investigation to understand ground risk)
- **Accept (Contingency):** Maintain contingency allowance for residual risks

## Quantitative Risk Analysis
Use Monte Carlo simulation (in tools like Primavera Risk Analysis) to model:
- P50 outcome (50th percentile — most likely)
- P80 outcome (80th percentile — prudent budget)

## PMBOK Risk Management Process
1. Plan Risk Management
2. Identify Risks
3. Perform Qualitative Risk Analysis
4. Perform Quantitative Risk Analysis
5. Plan Risk Responses
6. Implement Risk Responses
7. Monitor Risks

## Construction-Specific Risk Allowances
- **Contingency:** 5–15% of project value depending on design maturity
- **Risk Allowance:** 2–5% for known unknowns
- **Client Reserve:** 5–10% held by client for unknowns at project start
