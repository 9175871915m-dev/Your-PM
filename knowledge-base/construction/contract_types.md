# Construction Contract Types — NEC3, FIDIC, JCT

## Overview
Choosing the right contract form is one of the most important decisions in construction project management. The three dominant families in the UK and international markets are NEC, FIDIC, and JCT.

## NEC3 / NEC4 Contracts

### Key Features
- Collaborative, plain English drafting
- Strong emphasis on early warning and proactive management
- Programme is a contract document — contractor must submit and maintain updated programme
- Compensation Events (CE) mechanism for changes and risk events

### Main Contract Options
| Option | Type |
|---|---|
| Option A | Priced contract with Activity Schedule |
| Option B | Priced contract with Bill of Quantities |
| Option C | Target cost with Activity Schedule |
| Option D | Target cost with Bill of Quantities |
| Option E | Cost reimbursable |
| Option F | Management contract |

### Key Clauses
- **Clause 16:** Early Warning — contractor must notify PM of any risk within 7 days
- **Clause 60:** Compensation Events (CE) — 18 defined events
- **Clause 63:** Assessing Compensation Events
- **Clause 30:** Programme submission within 2 weeks of contract start

### NEC Best Practice
1. Always issue early warnings — failure reduces entitlement
2. Respond to Compensation Events within stated timeframes (default 3 weeks)
3. Keep programme updated — minimum 4-weekly submission

## FIDIC Contracts

### Key Features
- Internationally recognised — used globally on infrastructure and EPC projects
- More adversarial in nature than NEC
- Clear employer/contractor/engineer three-party structure
- Dispute resolution through DAB (Dispute Adjudication Board)

### Main Forms
| Form | Common Name | Use Case |
|---|---|---|
| Red Book (1999/2017) | Construction | Employer-designed civil works |
| Yellow Book (1999/2017) | Plant & Design-Build | Contractor designs plant/equipment |
| Silver Book (1999/2017) | EPC/Turnkey | Full contractor responsibility |
| Gold Book (2008) | DBO | Design, Build, Operate |

### Key FIDIC Clauses
- **Clause 8:** Programme — contractor submits within 28 days
- **Clause 13:** Variations and Adjustments
- **Clause 20:** Claims — written notice within 28 days of event
- **Clause 3:** Engineer's Authority — engineer acts impartially

## JCT Contracts

### Key Features
- Most widely used for UK building (not civil engineering) projects
- Developed by the Joint Contracts Tribunal
- More prescriptive and traditional than NEC

### Main Forms
| Form | Use Case |
|---|---|
| JCT Standard Building Contract (SBC) | Major projects, employer-designed |
| JCT Design and Build (D&B) | Contractor designs and builds |
| JCT Minor Works | Small projects under £250k |
| JCT Intermediate | Medium projects |
| JCT Management | Fast-track major projects |

### Key JCT Concepts
- **Architect/Contract Administrator** issues instructions and certificates
- **Practical Completion** triggers start of Defects Liability Period
- **Retention** — typically 5% reducing to 2.5% at Practical Completion
- **Liquidated and Ascertained Damages (LADs)** for programme delays

## Contract Selection Guide
| Factor | Recommended Contract |
|---|---|
| Collaborative culture needed | NEC3/NEC4 Option C or D |
| International infrastructure | FIDIC Red/Yellow Book |
| UK building, employer-designed | JCT SBC |
| Full contractor responsibility | FIDIC Silver Book or JCT D&B |
| Cost certainty | NEC Option A or JCT SBC Fixed Price |
| Low complexity | JCT Minor Works |
