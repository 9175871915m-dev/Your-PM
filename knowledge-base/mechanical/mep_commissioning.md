# MEP Coordination and Commissioning

## What is MEP Coordination?
MEP (Mechanical, Electrical, and Plumbing) coordination is the process of integrating all building services to ensure they are spatially coordinated, do not clash, and can be installed, maintained, and operated efficiently.

## MEP Coordination Process

### Stage 1: Design Coordination (Pre-Construction)
- All design consultants issue drawings in agreed format (typically Revit/BIM)
- Produce Combined Services Drawings (CSD) showing all MEP services together
- Conduct clash detection using Autodesk Navisworks or similar
- Agree zone allocation (ceiling void heights, riser locations, plantroom access)
- Issue Coordination Drawings for contractor use

### Stage 2: Shop Drawing Production
- Mechanical, Electrical, and Plumbing contractors produce fabrication-level drawings
- Ductwork layouts, pipe routing, containment routes, drainage runs
- Reviewed and approved by Design Manager or Lead Mechanical/Electrical Engineer

### Stage 3: Installation Sequence
- Agree MEP installation sequence — typically: containment → pipework → ductwork → electrical cabling → equipment → finishes
- Structural openings (floor penetrations, wall penetrations) coordinated early
- Maintain installation records for as-built purposes

## Commissioning Process

### What is Commissioning?
Commissioning is the systematic, documented verification that all installed MEP systems function as designed and meet the specified performance requirements.

### Phases of Commissioning
1. **Pre-Commissioning (Static Testing)**
   - Visual inspection of all installed equipment
   - Pressure testing of pipework systems (HVAC, domestic water, gas)
   - Insulation resistance (IR) tests on electrical cables
   - Continuity testing
   - Completion of Installation Verification Checklists (IVC)

2. **Commissioning (Dynamic Testing)**
   - Energise systems under controlled conditions
   - HVAC: Air balancing (TAB — Test, Adjust, Balance)
   - HVAC: Water balancing (hydronic systems)
   - Electrical: Load testing, protective device testing
   - BMS integration testing — control sequences verified

3. **Functional Performance Testing (FPT)**
   - Test complete sequences of operation
   - Verify against designed performance criteria (flow rates, temperatures, lux levels)
   - Witnessed by client, design team, and specialist commissioning engineer

4. **Handover**
   - Issue Commissioning Reports (air, water, electrical)
   - Provide O&M Manuals (Operation and Maintenance)
   - Provide As-Built Drawings
   - Conduct training for facilities management team

## HVAC Commissioning Specifics
- Air Handling Units (AHU): verify supply/return air volumes, fan speeds
- Fan Coil Units (FCU): zone-by-zone temperature setpoints
- Chiller plant: verify COP, leaving water temperature, condenser performance
- Cooling Towers: legionella risk assessment prior to fill
- VAV systems: verify modulation and zone controls

## Mechanical Pre-Commissioning Checklist
- [ ] All pipework pressure tested to 1.5× operating pressure
- [ ] Chemical flush and clean (BSRIA guide)
- [ ] Automatic controls powered and interlocked
- [ ] Insulation complete before commissioning
- [ ] Safety valves, pressure gauges, and drain points installed
- [ ] Water treatment programme in place

## Relevant Standards
- **BSRIA BG8/2009** — Commissioning water systems
- **BSRIA BG2/2010** — Commissioning air systems
- **CIBSE Commissioning Codes** — Series A, B, C, M, R, W
- **Building Regulations Part L** — Energy efficiency compliance
