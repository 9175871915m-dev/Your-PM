# HVAC Systems — Project Management Guide

## Overview
HVAC (Heating, Ventilation, and Air Conditioning) systems are among the most complex and cost-significant elements of a building services installation. Effective PM of HVAC works requires understanding of the system types, procurement strategy, programme sequence, and commissioning requirements.

## Common HVAC System Types

### Heating Systems
- **LPHW (Low Pressure Hot Water):** Most common in commercial buildings. Boiler plant circulates hot water via radiators, FCUs, or AHUs.
- **District Heating (DH):** Heat from a central energy centre distributed across multiple buildings.
- **Heat Pumps:** Air source (ASHP) or ground source (GSHP) — increasingly specified for low-carbon targets.
- **Underfloor Heating (UFH):** Embedded wet pipework in slab/screed. Install before finishes — critical programme milestone.

### Cooling Systems
- **Chilled Water (CHW):** Chiller plant produces chilled water for AHUs, FCUs, beam systems.
- **VRF/VRV Systems:** Variable Refrigerant Flow — direct expansion DX cooling to multiple indoor units. Ideal for partitioned office fit-out.
- **Precision Cooling (CRAC):** Data centre and server room cooling.

### Ventilation Systems
- **AHU (Air Handling Unit):** Central plant provides conditioned supply air via ductwork.
- **MVHR (Mechanical Ventilation Heat Recovery):** Recovers heat from exhaust air — required by Building Regulations Part F.
- **Local Extract Ventilation (LEV):** WCs, kitchens, server rooms.

## HVAC Programme Milestones
1. Schematic design agreed
2. Equipment selection and procurement (lead time for chillers: 20–30 weeks)
3. Plantroom structure complete
4. Primary plant installation
5. Secondary distribution (pipework, ductwork, containment)
6. Controls and BMS installation
7. Pre-commissioning and pressure testing
8. Commissioning and balancing
9. Functional performance tests
10. Practical completion

## Procurement Considerations
- **Long-lead items:** Chillers, AHUs, cooling towers — order at tender stage
- **Specialist subcontractors:** Ductwork fabrication, controls (BMS), insulation
- **Equipment room access:** Confirm all plant fits through access routes BEFORE delivery

## BMS (Building Management System)
The BMS monitors and controls all HVAC plant:
- setpoints, scheduling, fault alarms
- Typically Trend, Siemens Desigo, Johnson Controls, Honeywell
- Requires integration testing with each piece of plant
- BMS as-built graphics and operator training required at handover

## Key HVAC Performance Metrics
- Supply air temperature (heating: 35–45°C, cooling: 12–16°C off-coil)
- Room setpoint: typically 21°C heating / 24°C cooling dry-bulb
- Ventilation rates: CIBSE Guide A, Building Regulations Part F
- Noise criteria: NR35 in offices, NR25 in recording studios

## Legionella Risk Management
- All water systems must have a Legionella Risk Assessment before commissioning
- Cold water: maintain below 20°C
- Hot water: store at 60°C, distribute at 50°C
- Dead legs, low-use outlets, and calorifiers are high risk
- Responsible Person (RP) to be appointed and trained before handover
