# PMBOK Guide — Project Management Body of Knowledge

## Overview
The PMBOK Guide, published by PMI (Project Management Institute), is the global standard for project management. The 7th Edition (2021) shifted from process-based to principle-based guidance.

## PMBOK 7th Edition — 12 Principles
1. **Stewardship** — be a diligent, respectful, and caring steward
2. **Team** — create a collaborative project team environment
3. **Stakeholders** — effectively engage stakeholders
4. **Value** — focus on value delivery
5. **Systems Thinking** — recognise and respond to system interactions
6. **Leadership** — demonstrate leadership behaviours
7. **Tailoring** — tailor the approach based on context
8. **Quality** — build quality into processes and deliverables
9. **Complexity** — navigate complexity
10. **Risk** — optimise risk responses
11. **Adaptability** — embrace adaptability and resiliency
12. **Change** — enable change to achieve the envisioned future state

## PMBOK Performance Domains (7th Edition)
1. **Stakeholder** — engagement and communication
2. **Team** — high-performing teams
3. **Development Approach and Life Cycle**
4. **Planning** — iterative, progressive elaboration
5. **Project Work** — managing physical resources and procurements
6. **Delivery** — scope, requirements, quality
7. **Measurement** — metrics and KPIs
8. **Uncertainty** — risk, ambiguity, volatility

## PMBOK 6th Edition Process Groups (still widely used)
1. **Initiating** — Project Charter, stakeholder register
2. **Planning** — Scope, Schedule, Cost, Quality, Resource, Risk, Procurement, Communications
3. **Executing** — Direct and manage project work, quality assurance, team management
4. **Monitoring & Controlling** — Performance reports, change control, risk monitoring
5. **Closing** — Final acceptance, lessons learned, archiving

| Knowledge Area | Key Processes |

## Earned Value Management (EVM)
Key metrics for cost and schedule performance:
- **PV (Planned Value):** Budgeted cost for work scheduled
- **EV (Earned Value):** Budgeted cost for work performed
- **AC (Actual Cost):** Actual cost incurred
- **CPI (Cost Performance Index):** EV / AC (>1 = under budget)
- **SPI (Schedule Performance Index):** EV / PV (>1 = ahead of schedule)
- **CV (Cost Variance):** EV - AC
- **SV (Schedule Variance):** EV - PV
- **EAC (Estimate at Completion):** BAC / CPI
- **ETC (Estimate to Complete):** EAC - AC

## PMI Certifications
- **PMP (Project Management Professional)** — most recognised globally
- **CAPM** — entry level
- **PMI-RMP** — Risk Management Professional
- **PMI-ACP** — Agile Certified Practitioner
- **PgMP** — Program Management Professional

## When to Apply PMBOK
- Formal, structured projects with clear scope
- Regulated industries (Government, Infrastructure, Defence)
- When stakeholder governance and documentation are critical
- Projects above £500,000 in value
