01 / Foundations

What is a Project?

A project is a temporary endeavour undertaken to create a unique product, service, or result. It operates under the Iron Triangle — Scope, Time, and Cost — where changing one constraint forces a trade-off in the others.

Project

Temporary endeavour to create a unique product, service, or result.

Program

Related projects managed together to obtain benefits not available individually.

Portfolio

Projects and programs managed as a group to achieve strategic objectives.

Outcome

The end result; the broader intent of benefits and value delivered.

Value

Worth or usefulness as perceived by customers, organizations, and society.

Product

An artifact that is produced, quantifiable, and either an end item or component.

02 / PMBOK 7th Edition

The 12 Principles of Project Management

These principles guide professional behaviour and are aligned with the global values of responsibility, respect, fairness, and honesty.

01

Stewardship

Act responsibly with integrity, care, and trustworthiness — overseeing finances, resources, and the environment.

02

Team

Build collaborative environments with clear roles, behavioral norms, and shared ownership of outcomes.

03

Stakeholders

Engage proactively — analyse, communicate, and collaborate with all parties who can affect or are affected by the project.

04

Value

Shift focus from outputs (deliverables) to outcomes — the intended results and benefits those deliverables enable.

05

Systems Thinking

View the project holistically — a single change in one domain creates ripple effects across the entire system.

06

Leadership

Adapt your style from directive (chaos) to empowering (competent teams). Model honesty, integrity, and transparency.

07

Tailoring

Use "just enough" process matched to the project context — eliminate steps that add little value.

08

Quality

Focus on defect prevention rather than detection. Measure across performance, reliability, resilience, and sustainability.

09

Complexity

Navigate human behaviour, system interdependencies, ambiguity, and uncertainty through experimentation.

10

Risk

Continuously evaluate threats and opportunities. Navigate based on organisational risk appetite and thresholds.

11

Adaptability

Use short feedback loops, prototypes, and deferred decisions to absorb change and recover from setbacks.

12

Change

Prepare people for new behaviours with a structured transition — communicate vision early, reinforce post-implementation.
